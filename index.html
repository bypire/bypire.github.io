Multiphysics Hub

Coupled real-time simulation

An interactive application in which a single vehicle is driven in real time around an editable track while three coupled simulations are recomputed every frame. The vehicle is a four-wheel (double-track) model with Pacejka tyres and longitudinal and lateral load transfer. A two-dimensional fluid solver computes the flow around the body and returns the aerodynamic drag and downforce that act back on the tyres. A modal moving-load beam represents a bridge that deflects under the wheel load. Each model is first verified in Python against an analytical or finite-element reference, then reimplemented in JavaScript; the two implementations agree to about 10-13 for the vehicle and 10-9 metres for the bridge.

Bridge Weigh-in-Motion

Inverse problem and uncertainty quantification

An instrumented bridge can be used to weigh trucks in motion by inverting its measured bending response. This project implements the forward model, an Euler-Bernoulli beam under a moving, suspension-coupled vehicle load, and the recovery of the axle weights from the response. The recovery is treated as an ill-posed inverse problem: Tikhonov regularisation with L-curve parameter selection stabilises the closely spaced (tandem) axle case, and a Bayesian formulation returns a credible interval whose width is calibrated from the model error rather than assumed. The forward model is verified against the Frýba closed-form solution, and the structural-health component is validated against sixteen months of measured modal data from the KW51 bridge.

Optimal Racing Line

Trajectory optimisation and optimal control

The fastest line around a circuit is computed as the solution of a minimum-lap-time optimal-control problem rather than prescribed by hand. Taking arc length along the track as the independent variable removes the free final time, and the problem is transcribed to a nonlinear program by trapezoidal direct collocation with analytically derived Jacobians, then solved by sequential quadratic programming. On a 2.4 km circuit the optimised line is 8.1 percent faster than the geometric centreline and operates at the tyre grip limit for almost the entire lap. The result is verified against the closed-form skidpad lap time and confirmed to converge under mesh refinement.

Incompressible Flow Solver

Computational fluid dynamics

A two-dimensional incompressible Navier-Stokes solver built from first principles on a staggered (MAC) grid, using Chorin projection with a spectral pressure solve and a volume-penalisation immersed boundary for the cylinder. It reproduces the Ghia lid-driven cavity benchmark to a root-mean-square error of order 10-3, and it captures the von Kármán vortex street and the lock-in of vortex-induced vibration for an elastically mounted cylinder. The verified forward solver is then inverted into a vortex flow-meter that recovers the free-stream velocity from the measured shedding frequency.
